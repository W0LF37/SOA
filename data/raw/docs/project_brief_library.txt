Project Title:
Digital Library Management System

Project Overview:
A web-based system to manage library operations including book cataloging,
member borrowing and returns, seat reservations, and digital content access
for members and librarians.

Problem Statement:
Library staff currently manage book records and borrowing on paper-based
ledgers, leading to lost records, overdue tracking errors, and no visibility
into available inventory for members.

Proposed Solution:
Build a unified digital platform where members can search the catalog, borrow
books, and access digital resources, while librarians manage inventory,
overdue tracking, and member accounts from a single dashboard.

Target Users:
- Member
- Librarian

Main Features:
- Allow members to search and browse the book catalog by title, author, and genre
- Allow members to borrow physical books and return them with automated due date tracking
- Allow members to reserve books that are currently on loan by other members
- Allow members to access and read digital books and e-resources online
- Allow librarians to manage member accounts, book inventory, and overdue fines

Expected Benefits:
The system should be available during all library operating hours with no
unplanned downtime. All member records and borrowing history must be backed up
daily and recoverable within one hour in case of system failure.

Constraints or Special Notes:
- Must support barcode scanning for physical book check-in and check-out
- System must comply with member data privacy regulations
- Must support at least two hundred concurrent members during peak usage hours
